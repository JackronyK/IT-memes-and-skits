💥 “The Video That Shook My Wallet” 💥

On this day, nothing indicative of what was about to happen. So my friend—let’s call them J—shares a video titled “5 Lessons to Succeed Financially” by Jim Rohn. At first, I almost ignored it: Oh, hii video ya kiingereza mingi… just talk for talk’s sake. 🤦‍♂️ But I was wrong.

This wasn’t mere entertainment. It felt like a Damascus moment—my eyes were about to open to some bitter–yet–sweet truths. 🍋🍭 This video shouted the noble common sense that, if applied, can change anyone’s financial trajectory—especially in a time when most Kenyans are complaining of the wantam economics from bottom up. 📉📈

Core Principles

  • Quality over Quantity
    You don’t need dozens of tips—just a few actionable ideas you do every day.
  • Wisdom That Works
    If you’re tired of surviving and stagnating, you need practical wisdom, not vague inspiration.
  • Ideas + Action = Results
    Knowledge without implementation ni bure. Action ni moyo.

Lesson 1: Value Makes the Difference, Not Time

  1. Get Paid for Value, Not Hours
    • The marketplace rewards what you deliver, not how long you labor.
    • Effort alone won’t make you rich—value will.
    • Your bills, struggles, or good heart don’t matter to the market; your value does.
  2. Self-Assessment & Improvement
    • If you dislike your income, look in the mirror: “How can I increase my value?”
    • Replace waiting and wishing with learning, growing, and skill-building.
  3. Daily Value Investments
    • Read books, study your craft, practice relentlessly.
    • You don’t need fame—just excellence in something people will pay for.
“Don’t wish for better pay; wish to become a better person.”

Ways to Raise Your Value

  • Read field-specific books.
  • Solve common, costly problems.
  • Observe top performers—study their habits.
  • Practice consistently—repetition builds mastery.
  • Teach others—to teach is to learn twice.

Lesson 5: Environment Shapes Your Success

  1. Your Peer Group Matters
    • You rise to the level of the five people you spend the most time with.
    • Easy crowds keep you comfortable but stifle growth.
  2. Seek Growth-Focused Circles
    • Join groups where standards are high and conversations challenge you.
    • Find people speaking to your future, not your past.
  3. Practical Questions
    • Who are my top five?
    • Do they challenge or just agree?
    • Are they pulling me forward or dragging me back?
“Success is contagious—surround yourself with those who inspire growth.”
